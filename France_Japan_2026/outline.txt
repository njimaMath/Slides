1. What is universality
 Real world is mess
  - Stock prices fluctuate unpredictably
  - Bacteria colonies spread in irregular patterns
  - Traffic jams form and dissolve on complex road networks

 Probability captures an underlying structure, so called universality
  - Despite the apparent complexity and difference of models, their large-scale behavior is governed by the same mathematical law
  - Famous classical examples:  Central Limit Theorem
    * Consider coin flips, dice rolls, measurement errors, ...
    * After rescaling, the sum converges to the Gaussian distribution
