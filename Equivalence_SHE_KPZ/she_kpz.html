Higher-dimensional KPZ is a central non-integrable example.

KPZ equation

Fix $\beta>0$. The following is called the KPZ equation:

For $t\in[0,\infty)$ and $x\in\R^d$,

$$\begin{aligned} \partial_t h(t,x)&=\frac{1}{2}\Delta h(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2+\beta \xi(t,x)\\ &= \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=1}^d \partial_{x_i}^2 h(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\sum_{i=1}^d (\partial_{x_i} h(t,x))^2+\beta \xi(t,x), \end{aligned}$$

where $\xi(t,x)$ is a space-time white noise on $[0,\infty)\times \R^d$.

The meaning of each term

$$\partial_t h(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\Delta h(t,x)}_{\text{relaxation}}+\frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2}_{\text{lateral growth}}+\underbrace{\beta \xi(t,x)}_{\text{noise}}$$
$\Delta h(t,x)$: relaxation

The meaning of each term

$$\partial_t h(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\Delta h(t,x)}_{\text{relaxation}}+\frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2}_{\text{lateral growth}}+\underbrace{\beta \xi(t,x)}_{\text{noise}}$$
$\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2$: lateral growth

The meaning of each term

$$\partial_t h(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\Delta h(t,x)}_{\text{relaxation}}+\frac{1}{2}\underbrace{\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2}_{\text{lateral growth}}+\underbrace{\beta \xi(t,x)}_{\text{noise}}$$
$\beta \xi(t,x)$: noise

Problem of the construction of solutions to the KPZ eq.

KPZ equation
$$\partial_t h(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta h(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2+\beta \xi(t,x).$$
Problem
  • $\xi$ is not a function $\Rightarrow$ $h(t,x)$ may not be a function.
  • How to make sense of $\left|\nabla h(t,x)\right|^2$?
Scheme
  • Mollify the noise.
  • Consider a (smooth) solution to the mollified KPZ equation.
  • We switch off the mollification and consider the limit of solutions.

Regularization scheme

Let $\phi$ be a smooth and compactly supported function on $\R^d$ satisfying $\int_{\R^2}\phi(t,x){\rm d}t{\rm d}x=1$.

Mollify the white noise $\xi(t,x)$ in space on scale $\e$:

$$\xi^{\e} (t,x):= \int \phi^\e(x-y) \xi(t,y) {\rm d} y\Rightarrow \xi(t,x),$$

where $\phi^{\e}(x):= \e^{-d} \phi(\e^{-1} x)$.

Let us consider:

$$\partial_t h_{\e}(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta h_{\e}(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h_{\e}(t,x)\right|^2+\beta \xi^{\e}(t,x)$$

Q. $h_{\epsilon}$ converges as $\e\to 0$?

A. No! We have to modify the equation more.

Regularization scheme

Let $\phi$ be a smooth and compactly supported function on $\R^d$ satisfying $\int_{\R^2}\phi(t,x){\rm d}t{\rm d}x=1$.

Mollify the white noise $\xi(t,x)$ in space on scale $\e$:

$$\xi^{\e} (t,x):= \int \phi^\e(x-y) \xi(t,y) {\rm d} y,$$

where $\phi^{\e}(x):= \e^{-d} \phi(\e^{-1} x)$.

We introduce new parameters and consider the regularized KPZ:

$$\partial_t h_{\e}(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta h_{\e}(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h_{\e}(t,x)\right|^2+\beta_{\e} \xi^{\e}(t,x)-C_{\e}.$$

Can we take $\beta_{\epsilon}$ and $C_{\epsilon}$ so that $h_{\epsilon}$ converges as $\e\to 0$?

Choice of $\beta_{\epsilon}$ and $C_{\epsilon}$

Recall that $\xi^{\e} (t,x):= \int \phi^\e(x-y) \xi(t,y) {\rm d} y$.

The choice of $\beta_{\epsilon}$

For fixed $\hat{\beta}\in(0,\infty)$, we choose

$$ {\color{red} \beta_{\epsilon}}=\begin{cases} \hat{\beta}& \text{($d=1$)} \\ \hat{\beta}{\color{purple}\sqrt{\frac{2\pi}{\log \e^{-1}}}}& \text{($d=2$)} \\ \hat{\beta}{\color{purple} \e^{\frac{d-2}{2}}}& \text{($d\geq 3$)} . \end{cases} $$
The choice of $C_{\epsilon}$

We choose ${\color{red}C_{\epsilon}}=\frac{1}{2}\beta_{\epsilon}^2 \e^{-d} \|\phi\|_{L^2}^2$.

Remark
  • There are other choices (e.g., Family-Vicsek scaling).
  • This choice is related to directed polymers explained later.

Previous researches ($d=1$)

We consider the solution $h_{\epsilon}$ to the mollified KPZ:

$$\partial_t h_{\e}(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta h_{\e}(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h_{\e}(t,x)\right|^2+\beta_{\e} \xi^{\e}(t,x)-C_{\e}.$$
Theorem [Bertini-Giacomin, CMP 1997]

When $d=1$, for any $\hat{\beta}\geq 0$, $h_{\epsilon}(t,x)\to \exists h(t,x)$ as $\e\to 0$.

Law of large numbers of $u_{\epsilon}$ for $d\geq 3$

Recall that $u_{\epsilon}$ is the solution to $\partial_t u_{\e}=\frac{1}{2}\Delta u_{\e}+\beta_{\e} u_{\epsilon}\xi^{\e}.$

Let $u_{\epsilon}(0,\cdot)\equiv u_0(\cdot)\in \mathcal{C}_b(\R^d)$. We define $\bar{u}(t,x)=E_x^{\rm BM}[u_0(B_t)]$.

Theorem [Mukherjee-Shamov-Zeitouni, ECP 2016; Cosco-Nakajima-Nakashima, SPA 2022]
  1. For all $\hat{\beta}<\hat{\beta}_c$, $f\in\mathcal{C}_c(\R^d)$ and $u_0\in \mathcal{C}_b(\R^d)$, as $\e\to 0$, $$\int_{\R^d} f(x) u_{\epsilon}(t,x){\rm d} x\overset{L^1}{\rightarrow} \int_{\R^d} f(x) \bar{u}(t,x){\rm d} x.$$
  2. For all $\hat{\beta}> \hat{\beta}_c$, $f\in\mathcal{C}_c(\R^d)$ and $u_0\in \mathcal{C}_b(\R^d)$, as $\e\to 0$, $$u_{\epsilon}(t,x)\overset{P}{\rightarrow}0.$$

Law of large numbers of $u_{\epsilon}$ for $d\geq 3$

Theorem [Mukherjee-Shamov-Zeitouni, ECP 2016; Cosco-Nakajima-Nakashima, SPA 2022]
  1. For all $\hat{\beta}<\hat{\beta}_c$, $f\in\mathcal{C}_c(\R^d)$ and $u_0\in \mathcal{C}_b(\R^d)$, as $\e\to 0$, $$\int_{\R^d} f(x) u_{\epsilon}(t,x){\rm d} x\overset{L^1}{\rightarrow} \int_{\R^d} f(x) \bar{u}(t,x){\rm d} x.$$
  2. For all $\hat{\beta}> \hat{\beta}_c$ and $u_0 \in \mathcal{C}_b(\R^d)$, as $\e\to 0$, $$u_{\epsilon}(t,x)\overset{P}{\rightarrow}0.$$
The limit of $u_{\epsilon}$ (also $h_{\epsilon}$) is just a function (NOT random)!

Fluctuation of $u_{\epsilon}$ (SHE)

Recall that for all $\hat{\beta}<\hat{\beta}_c$,

$$\int_{\R^d} f(x) u_{\epsilon}(t,x){\rm d} x\overset{L^1}{\rightarrow} \int_{\R^d} f(x) \bar{u}(t,x){\rm d} x.$$
Theorem [GRZ, CMP 2018; Cosco-Nakajima-Nakashima, SPA 2022]

Suppose $u_{\epsilon}(0,\cdot)\equiv u_0\in \mathcal{C}_b$. For all $\hat{\beta}<\hat{\beta}_{L^2}$,

$$\e^{-\frac{d-2}{2}}\int_{\R^d} f(x) (u_{\epsilon}(t,x)-\bar{u}(t,x)){\rm d} x\overset{(d)}{\rightarrow} \int f(x) \mathcal{U}_1(t,x){\rm d} x,$$

where $\mathcal{U}_1(t,x)$ is the solution of $\mathcal{U}_1(0,x)\equiv 0$ and

$$\partial_t \mathcal{U}_1(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta \mathcal{U}_1(t,x)+\gamma(\hat{\beta})\bar{u}(t,x)\xi(t,x).\text{ (EW equation)}$$

Note that $\gamma(\hat{\beta})\to \infty$ as $\hat{\beta}\to \hat{\beta}_{L^2}$.

Fluctuation of $h_{\epsilon}$ (KPZ)

Let $h_{\epsilon}(t,x):=\log{u_{\epsilon}(t,x)}$.

Theorem [Magnen-Unterberger, JSP 2018; Dunlap-Gu-Ryzhik-Zeitouni, PTRF 2020; Lygkonis-Zygouras, AIHP 2022; Cosco-Nakajima-Nakashima, SPA 2022]

Suppose $\|\log{u_0}\|_\infty<\infty$. For all $\hat{\beta}<\hat{\beta}_{L^2}$,

$$\e^{-\frac{d-2}{2}}\int_{\R^d} f(x) (h_{\epsilon}(t,x)-\E h_{\epsilon}(t,x)){\rm d} x\overset{(d)}{\rightarrow} \int f(x) \mathcal{U}_2(t,x){\rm d} x,$$

where $\mathcal{U}_2(t,x)$ is the solution of $\mathcal{U}_2(0,x)\equiv 0$ and

$$\partial_t \mathcal{U}_2(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta \mathcal{U}_2(t,x)+\nabla\log{\bar{u}(t,x)}\cdot \nabla \mathcal{U}_2(t,x) + \gamma(\hat{\beta})\xi(t,x).$$

KPZ $\Rightarrow$ Directed Polymer (via Feynman-Kac formula)

We define $u_{\epsilon}(t,x):=\exp{(h_{\epsilon}(t,x))}$, $V(x):= \int \phi(x-y)\phi(y) {\rm d} y$, and $C_{\epsilon}:=\frac{1}{2}\beta_{\epsilon}^2\e^{-d}V(0)$.

Assume $h_\epsilon(0,x)=0$, equivalently $u_\epsilon(0,x)=1$.

Proposition

The following are equivalent:

  • $\partial_t h_{\e}(t,x)=\frac{1}{2}\Delta h_{\e}(t,x)+\frac{1}{2}\left|\nabla h_{\e}(t,x)\right|^2+\beta_{\e} \xi^{\e}(t,x)-C_{\e}$
  • $\partial_t u_{\epsilon}=\frac{1}{2}\Delta u_{\epsilon} +\beta_{\epsilon} \xi^{\e} (t,x)u_{\epsilon}-C_{\epsilon}u_{\epsilon}$.

If $u_\e$ is the solution to the above, then $u_{\epsilon}$ can be written as

$$\begin{aligned} u_{\epsilon}(t,x)&= {\rm E}_{x}^{\rm BM}\left[\exp{\left(\beta_{\epsilon}\int_{0}^t \xi^{\e} (t-s,B_s)ds-C_{\epsilon}t\right)}\right]\\ &\overset{d}{=}{\rm E}_{x}^{\rm BM}\left[\exp{\underbrace{\left(\beta_{\epsilon}\int_{0}^t \xi^{\e} (s,B_s)ds-C_{\epsilon}t\right)}_{\text{Hamiltonian of a directed polymer}}}\right]. \end{aligned}$$

Some remarks

  • We discuss a relation between SHE and KPZ equation in weak disorder regime.
  • Our main result is only stated in discrete directed polymer.
  • From now on, we only consider the discrete model.
  • However, we believe that the same techniques work for continuum models.

Discrete polymer model

  • Let $(\omega(n,x))_{n\in\N,x\in\Z^d}$ be i.i.d. random variables.
  • We assume that the distribution of $\omega(n,x)$ has a compact support.
  • Let $\lambda(\beta):=\log \E[e^{\beta \omega(n,x)}]\in\R$ for any $\beta\in\R$.
  • Let us define the partition function $Z_N(x)$ for $x\in\Z^d$ of directed polymers: $$Z_N(x):=Z_{\omega,N,\beta}(x):={\rm E}_x\left[e^{\beta\sum_{k=1}^N\omega(k,X_k)-N\lambda(\beta)}\right],$$ where ${\rm E}_x$ denotes the expectation of the simple random walk starting at $x$.
  • For simplicity of notation, we write $Z_N:=Z_N(0)$.
  • We call $\{\beta\geq 0|~\P(\liminf_N Z_N>0)=1\}$ the weak disorder regime.

Discrete Polymer vs. Continuum Polymer

Recall that

  • $u_\epsilon (t,x) \overset{d}{=}\mathrm{E}^{\text{BM}}\left[\exp\left(\hat{\beta}\int^T_0 \phi \ast \xi(s,B_s)ds - C_\epsilon\right)\right]$ with $T=\epsilon^{-2}$ and $C_\epsilon=\frac{1}{2}\hat{\beta}^2TV(0)$.
  • $h_\epsilon(t,x)=\log{u_\epsilon (t,x)}$ solves $\partial_t h=\frac{1}{2}\Delta_x h+\frac{1}{2}|\nabla_x h|^2+\beta_\epsilon \xi^\epsilon-C_\epsilon$.

Tail exponent

Let $p_*(\beta):= \sup\{p\geq 0|~ \sup_N\E[Z_N(\beta)^p]<\infty\}$ (tail exponent).

Theorem [Junk, AOP 2025]

In the weak disorder regime, it holds

$$p_*(\beta)\geq \frac{d+2}{d}.$$

Moreover, for a certain class of weight distribution (finite support),

$$\beta<\beta_c\Leftrightarrow p_*(\beta)> \frac{d+2}{d}.$$

Fluctuation for SHE in weak disorder regime

We define $p_*:=p_*(\beta)$ as before.

Let $\xi:=\xi(\beta):=- \frac{d}{2}+\frac{d+2}{2(p_*\wedge 2)}$ (fluctuation exponent).

Theorem [Junk, AOP 2025]

Given a compactly supported function $f:\R^d\to \R$, we define the fluctuation of discrete SHE as

$$\chi_N(f):= N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) (Z_N(x)-\E Z_N(x)).$$

In the weak disorder regime, for any $\e>0$ and $f\not\equiv 0$, it holds

$$\lim_{N\to\infty}\P( N^{-\xi-\e}<|\chi_N(f)|< N^{-\xi+\e})= 1 .$$

Equivalence of fluctuation between SHE and KPZ

From now on, we assume $p_*(\beta)>\frac{d+2}{d}$.

Given a compactly supported function $f:\R^d\to \R$, we define the fluctuation of discrete KPZ as

$$\kappa_N(f):= N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) (\log{Z_N(x)}-\E \log{Z_N(x)}).$$
Theorem [Junk-N, PMP 2025]

There exists $\delta>0$ such that

$$\lim_{N\to\infty}\mathbb{P}(|\chi_N(f)-\kappa_N(f)|\leq N^{-\xi-\delta})=1.$$
Remark

This kind of claim also appears in proofs in the $L^2$ regime in other papers.

Main consequence

Theorem [Junk-N, PMP 2025]

There exists $\delta>0$ such that

$$\lim_{N\to\infty}\mathbb{P}(|\chi_N(f)-\kappa_N(f)|\leq N^{-\xi-\delta})=1.$$

Together with the result of [Junk, AOP 2025] ($|\chi_N(f)|=N^{-\xi+o(1)}$), we conclude:

Corollary [Junk-N, PMP 2025]

There exists $\delta>0$ such that

$$\lim_{N\to\infty}\P\left(\frac{|\chi_N(f)-\kappa_N(f)|}{\min\{|\chi_N(f)|,|\kappa_N(f)|\}}\leq N^{-\delta}\right)=1.$$

Why surprising

  • For $\beta\in(\beta_2,\beta_c)$, it is believed that $$ N^\xi \chi_N(f):= N^{\xi-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) (Z_N(x)-\E Z_N(x)) \to {\rm Stable}.$$
  • We can prove that $$\begin{aligned} &\bullet \sup_{N\in\N} \E[(\log Z_N)^2]<\infty\\ & \bullet \lim_{|x-y|\to\infty}\sup_{N\in\N} {\rm Cov}(\log Z_N(x), \log Z_N(y))=0. \end{aligned}$$
  • Hence I originally expected that $$N^\xi \kappa_N(f):= N^{\xi-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) (\log Z_N(x)-\E \log Z_N(x)) \to {\rm Gauss}.$$
Conjecture

For $\beta\in(\beta_2,\beta_c)$, the KPZ fluctuation satisfies

$$N^\xi \kappa_N(f)\overset{(d)}{\longrightarrow}{\rm Stable}.$$

Outline of the proof

Let ${\rm E}^{\leftarrow}_{t,y}$ denotes expectation for a backward simple random walk $(\overleftarrow{X}_i)_{i=s}^{t}$ with $\overleftarrow{X}_t=y$. For $s < t$, define the backward partition function ending at $(t,y)$ by

$$\overleftarrow{Z}_{[s,t)}((t,y)):={\rm E}^{\leftarrow}_{t,y}\left[\exp\left(\beta\sum_{i=s}^{t-1}\omega(i,\overleftarrow{X}_i)-(t-s)\lambda(\beta)\right)\right],$$

Let $\ell_k:=k^{1/8}$ and $E_k(y):=e^{\beta \omega(k,y)-\lambda(\beta)}-1$. We define

$$\rho_N(f):= N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N})\left[\sum_{k=1}^{N} \sum_{y\in \Z^d} \overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))E_k(y) p_N(x,y)\right].$$

We will prove that

$$\begin{aligned} &\chi_N(f)\approx \rho_N(f)\quad\text{(Approximation for SHE)},\\ &\kappa_N(f)\approx \rho_N(f)\quad\text{(Approximation for KPZ)}. \end{aligned}$$

Sketch proof (Approximation for SHE)

Let $\ell_k:=k^{1/8}$ and $E_k(y):=e^{\beta \omega(k,y)-\lambda(\beta)}-1$. We have the following approximation for $\chi_N$:

$$\begin{aligned} \chi_N(f)&=N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) (Z_N(x)-1)\\ &= N^{-d/2}\sum_{y\in \Z^d}\sum_{k=1}^{N}E_k(y) \left[\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) Z_{k}(x,y) p_k(x,y)\right]\\ &\approx N^{-d/2}\sum_{y\in \Z^d}\sum_{k=1}^{N} E_k(y)\left[\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) \overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))p_N(x,y)\right]=\rho_N(f), \end{aligned}$$

where we have used law of large numbers (similar to $Z_k\approx Z_{\ell_k}$).

Some ingredients

As before, we assume $p_*(\beta)>\frac{d+2}{d}$.

The following play an important role in the proof below:

Theorem [Junk, JEMS 2025]

Let $Z_N(x,y):={\rm E}^x[e^{\beta\sum_{k=1}^{N-1}\omega(k,X_k)-(N-1)\lambda(\beta)}|~X_N=y]$. Let $\ell_N:=N^{1/8}$. For any $p\lt p_*(\beta)$ and $c>0$, it holds

$$\lim_{N\to\infty}\sup_{x,y\in [-N^{1-c},N^{1-c}]}\E|Z_N(x,y)-Z_{\ell_N}(x) \overleftarrow{Z}_{[N-\ell_N,N)}((N,y))|^p=0.$$
Theorem [Junk-N, PMP 2025]

There exists $C=C(\beta)>0$ such that for any $u\geq 1$ and $N\in\N$,

$$\mathbb{P}(Z_{N}<1/u)\leq C e^{-(\log{u})^2/C}.$$

Some ingredients

It holds:

$$\begin{aligned} &\lim_{N\to\infty}\sup_{x,y\in [-N^{1-c},N^{1-c}]}\E|Z_N(x,y)-Z_{\ell_N}(x) \overleftarrow{Z}_{[N-\ell_N,N)}((N,y))|^p=0,\\ &\mathbb{P}(Z_{N}<1/u)\leq C e^{-(\log{u})^2/C}. \end{aligned}$$

We define the polymer measure:

$$\mu_{N}(x,y):= \frac{{\rm E}_x\left[e^{\beta\sum_{k=1}^{N-1}\omega(k,X_k)-(N-1)\lambda(\beta)}\mathbf{1}_{X_N=y}\right]}{Z_{N-1}(x)}=\frac{Z_N(x,y)p_N(x,y)}{Z_{N-1}(x)}.$$

By $Z_k(x,y)\approx Z_{\ell_k}(x)\overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))$ and $Z_{\ell_k}(x), Z_k(x)\to Z_\infty(x)$, they imply

$$\mu_k(x,y)=\frac{Z_k(x,y)p_k(x,y)}{Z_{k-1}(x)}\approx \frac{Z_{\ell_k}(x)\overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))p_k(x,y)}{Z_{k-1}(x)} \approx \overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))p_k(x,y).$$

Sketch proof (Approximation for KPZ I)

For $\kappa_N$, we consider the following Martingale decomposition:

$$\begin{aligned} & \kappa_N(f)= N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}f(x/\sqrt{N}) (\log{Z_N(x)}-\E[\log{Z_N(x)}])\\ &=N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}\sum_{k=1}^N f(x/\sqrt{N}) (\log{\frac{Z_k(x)}{Z_{k-1}(x)}}-\E[\log{\frac{Z_k(x)}{Z_{k-1}(x)}}|~\mathcal{F}_{k-1}])\\ &\quad+N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d} \sum_{k=1}^N f(x/\sqrt{N}) (\E[\log{\frac{Z_k(x)}{Z_{k-1}(x)}}|~\mathcal{F}_{k-1}]-\E[\log{\frac{Z_k(x)}{Z_{k-1}(x)}}])\\ &\approx N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d} \sum_{k=1}^N f(x/\sqrt{N}) \sum_{y\in \Z^d} \mu_{k}(x,y) E_k(y). \end{aligned}$$

where $\mu_k(x,y):=\frac{Z_k(x,y)p_k(x,y)}{Z_{k-1}(x)},\, E_k(y):=e^{\beta \omega(k,y)-\lambda(\beta)}-1$ and used $$\log{\frac{Z_k(x)}{Z_{k-1}(x)}}= \log{\Big(1+\sum_{y\in \Z^d} \mu_{k}(x,y) E_k(y)\Big)}\approx \sum_{y\in \Z^d} \mu_{k}(x,y) E_k(y).$$

Sketch proof (Approximation for KPZ II)

Let $\ell_k:=k^{1/8}$ and $E_k(y):=e^{\beta \omega(k,y)-\lambda(\beta)}-1$. Recall that

$$\mu_k(x,y) \approx \overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))p_k(x,y).$$

Hence, we have the following approximation for $\kappa_N$:

$$\begin{aligned} \kappa_N(f)&\approx N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d}\sum_{k=1}^N f(x/\sqrt{N}) \sum_{y\in \Z^d} \mu_{k}(x,y) E_k(y)\\ &\approx N^{-d/2}\sum_{x\in \Z^d} \sum_{k=1}^{N} f(x/\sqrt{N})\sum_{y\in \Z^d} \left[ \overleftarrow{Z}_{[k-\ell_k,k)}((k,y))E_k(y) p_N(x,y)\right]=\rho_N(f). \end{aligned}$$

Therefore, we have $\chi_N(f)\approx \kappa_N(f)$.
